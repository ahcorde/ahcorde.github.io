Hi. I'm Alejandro Hernández Cordero.

Open Source Robotics contributor which solve robotics and simulation problems efficiently

I’m a tech-hungry roboticist, from the first day of my professional life I have been designing, building, and programming robotic hardware. As part of past projects, I have had the opportunity to develop algorithms, libraries and hardware for drones, autonomous vehicles and robotic arms. From this experience, I have gained a broad understanding of robotic technologies and have experience developing complex robotic systems.

I have 8+ years of experience in developing, maintaining and contributing to open source projects for robotics. I am fluent in C++, the language of robotics, and Python and the most common techniques for their interoperation. For example, I was involved in rclpy, gz-math, gz-sim and sdformat Python bindings. I develop multiple project using ROS and ROS 2 for multiple clients. I have designed/contributed/fixed extensively in Gazebo and Gazebo-classic. I created the gz-omni project which allows Gazebo and Issac Sim to interoperate together.

I’ve always rooted and advocated for open-source development and I have been lucky enough for having the possibility to embrace this development approach throughout my career so far
